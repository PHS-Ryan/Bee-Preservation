Practicing Kaitiakitanga

How to be a guardian

Practicing Kaitiakitanga means caring for bees by supporting their environment. In Auckland, this can include planting flowers like manuka and harakeke, avoiding harmful pesticides and protecting bees nests by making sure they are out of reach from predators.

The threats

One of the biggest threats to bees is the use of harmful pesticides, which can poison bees and disrupt their ability to navigate. Habitat loss due to city development and also reduces the amount of flowers and hives. Climate change is another major threat, as it affects the blooming times of plants and affects bee behavior.

How to get involved

In New Zealand, there are many ways we can help protect bees and support their survival. One of the best things we can do is plant bee friendly flowers like manuka, clover, and lavender in our gardens or schools, which give bees food. Avoiding harmful sprays and chemicals is also important, especially during the day when bees are most active. Supporting local honey and beekeepers helps keep small, healthy bee colonies going. Joining community gardens or local conservation groups that care for bees and provide suitable spaces for bees to thrive is another way you can help bees thrive and help them help us.
